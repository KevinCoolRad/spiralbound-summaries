Vintage Fruitcake Recipe – A Modern Comeback

It’s time for fruitcake to make a comeback. Or maybe a rebrand. A glow-up. And it totally deserves one.

This version is adapted from a 1977 spiral-bound community cookbook — “More Saratoga Secrets Shared.” The original is great, but the ingredients? Well, they needed a refresh. Enter golden raisins, real pistachios, and the finest cherries money can buy.

The Fruit Upgrade

No more neon candied fruit. If you want a shortcut, the King Arthur Fruitcake Fruit Blend is a clean and flavorful option that’s free from artificial colors and packed with dates, currants, and cranberries.

Want to make your own candied fruit? Do it! You’ll thank yourself.

Cherry on Top

The best upgrade of all: cherries. Skip the dyed-red jars and splurge on Luxardo Maraschino Cherries. They’re bold, rich, and syrupy — the kind of cherry you don’t hide inside a cake, you showcase it.

The Recipe

Below is the fully modernized version of the original “Favorite Fruitcake.” The method is the same, but the ingredients now reflect today’s cleaner, tastier choices.

Favorite Fruitcake

  • 1-1/2 c. chopped candied cherries (I used Luxardo)
  • 1 c. chopped candied pineapple
  • 1/2 c. citron, 1/2 c. lemon peel, 1/2 c. orange peel
  • 1 c. golden raisins
  • 1 c. chopped pistachios (instead of walnuts)
  • 1 c. flour (for fruit)
  • 1 c. butter, 1 c. sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/4 c. honey (instead of corn syrup)
  • 1/4 c. orange juice
  • 1/4 c. Fireball or sherry
  • 2 c. flour (for batter)

Bake at 275°F in greased pans. See times above. Cool completely before removing.

Final Thoughts

This cake smells like holiday nostalgia. It’s rich, gooey, and nothing like the dry store-bought bricks of decades past. Give fruitcake another try — it might just become your new holiday tradition.
