Vintage Zucchini Patties

From the pages of a spiral-bound community cookbook

Looking for a way to use up extra zucchini? This vintage recipe for zucchini patties might be the answer. It’s one of those old-school, crowd-pleasing dishes that works as a light lunch, a side dish, or even an appetizer. It originally came from a community cookbook, which means it’s been tested and loved by many home cooks over the years.

These patties are soft on the inside and crispy on the outside, with fresh zucchini, egg, and onion bound together by breadcrumbs and flour. You can customize them with cheese, herbs, or spices — or leave them as-is for a classic take.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups grated zucchini
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Grate zucchini and squeeze out excess moisture using a clean towel or paper towel.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine zucchini, eggs, onion, flour, breadcrumbs, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  4. Scoop heaping tablespoons of the mixture into the pan and flatten slightly.
  5. Fry until golden brown on both sides, about 3–4 minutes per side.
  6. Remove and drain on paper towels. Serve warm with sour cream, herbs, or a squeeze of lemon.

These patties are delicious hot out of the pan or served cold the next day. If you're swimming in zucchini, double the batch and freeze extras for later!

Recipe courtesy of a 1984 Midwestern church community cookbook.
